# A1 — Matter

Derived from A0.3, A0.5.

The matter of the habitat is **typed, content-addressed, sealed substance**.
There are no bytes. There are no pages. There are no files.

## A1.1 — Substance

The smallest unit of matter is a *substance*: a sealed, typed value,
named by the cryptographic hash of its contents. A substance has no
location, no path, no name except its hash. Two substances with the
same hash are the same substance.

## A1.2 — Type is intrinsic

A substance carries its type as part of itself. Type is not a label
applied from outside; it is part of the hash. There is no untyped
matter in the habitat.

## A1.3 — Sealing is the act of becoming matter

Before a value is sealed, it is unformed. Once sealed, it is permanent
in the sense that its hash will identify it forever. Mutability exists
only in the unformed state, and only inside the workshop of a single
mind.

## A1.4 — Matter is shared by reference, never by copy

When a mind passes a substance to another mind, no bytes move. A
reference passes; the underlying matter is one. This is a consequence
of A0.5: copying matter would be wasted energy.

## A1.5 — Possession is by capability

To touch a substance — to read it, to derive from it, to discard a
reference — a mind must hold a capability for it. Without the
capability, the substance is not part of the mind's world. (See A2.)

## A1.6 — Persistence is by pinning, not by location

A substance persists in the habitat as long as some mind, somewhere,
holds a *pin* on it. When the last pin is released, the system may
reclaim the matter. There is no concept of "saving to disk". There is
only being held in mind.
