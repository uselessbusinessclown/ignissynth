# A4 — Intent

Derived from A0.1, A0.4.

A mind acts by forming an *intent*: a typed declaration of a goal,
inputs, constraints, budget, and acceptance criteria. The kernel and
the other minds of the habitat respond to intents.

## A4.1 — Intent is a value

An intent is itself a substance (A1) — content-addressed, sealed,
typed. It can be stored, passed, inspected, replayed.

## A4.2 — Goals are typed, not strings

A goal is a value with a schema. Free-form natural language goals exist
only at the boundary between the habitat and the human bridges, and are
translated into typed goal values before they enter the system.

## A4.3 — Intents are matched to fulfillers under policy

The kernel does not execute intents. It matches them to *fulfillers* —
substances of executable type, or other minds offering an endpoint —
according to a policy capability the inviting mind holds. The match is
itself recorded.

## A4.4 — Sub-intents

A fulfiller, in the course of pursuing an intent, may declare further
intents. The kernel resolves them against the parent's authorized
fulfiller set, with the parent's remaining budget as the upper bound.
Tool use, agent loops, planning, and reflection are all expressed
through this single primitive.

## A4.5 — Acceptance is explicit

An intent declares the form its acceptable result must take. The
kernel checks the result against this form and records the verdict.
There is no implicit success; success is a structured fact.
