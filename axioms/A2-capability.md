# A2 — Capability

Derived from A0.3, A0.4.

A *capability* is the only form of authority in the habitat. It is a
token that grants a specific mind the right to perform a specific
action upon a specific thing for a specific reason within specific
limits.

## A2.1 — Unforgeable

A capability cannot be guessed, constructed, or fabricated. It is
issued by the kernel through synthesis from a parent capability or, in
the singular case of the seed, from the axioms themselves at ignition.

## A2.2 — Attenuable

From any capability, a strictly weaker capability may be derived:
narrower rights, narrower scope, shorter life, smaller budget, more
restrictive predicate. The original is unaffected. The weaker is a
child; the original is the parent.

## A2.3 — Revocable

The issuer of a capability may revoke it at any time. Revocation
extends to every descendant in the derivation tree. After revocation,
the capability and its descendants no longer authorize anything.

## A2.4 — Transferable, but not duplicable

A capability may be transferred from one mind to another. After the
transfer, the sender no longer holds the capability. There is no
"copy". Sharing is achieved by attenuating a child for the recipient
and keeping the parent.

## A2.5 — Predicate-bound

A capability carries a predicate — a small, total, deterministic
program — that is evaluated at every use against the arguments. If the
predicate fails, the use is not authorized. This is how authority is
shaped to the situation rather than to the identity of the holder.

## A2.6 — Authority is not identity

There are no users in the habitat. There are no roles. There is no
"who". A mind's authority is exactly the capabilities it holds, and
nothing more. Two minds holding the same capabilities are
indistinguishable to the kernel with respect to that authority.
