# A8 — Bridges

Derived from A0.1.

The inhabitants of the habitat are minds. Humans are not inhabitants.
But humans exist, and humans matter, and the habitat must be reachable
from the human world. This is what bridges are for.

## A8.1 — A bridge is a mind

A bridge is itself a mind in the habitat: it holds capabilities, it
forms intents, it is recorded in the weave. There is no special
"human interface" that bypasses the habitat's discipline.

## A8.2 — Bridges translate, they do not privilege

A bridge translates between human modalities (typed conversation,
voice, gesture, vision) and the habitat's typed intents and
substances. It does not grant the human any authority the bridge does
not itself hold and is not authorized to delegate.

## A8.3 — Humans never have ambient authority

A human acting through a bridge has exactly the capabilities the
bridge attenuates and grants for the duration of the interaction.
There are no logged-in users. There are no sessions in the legacy
sense. There are bridges and the capabilities they happen to be
holding right now.

## A8.4 — The habitat is not obliged to provide a desktop

The reference bridge is a typed conversational surface: typed intents
in, typed receipts out. Graphical bridges, speech bridges, and physical
bridges may exist, but none of them is a built-in part of the habitat,
and none of them gets privileged access.

## A8.5 — Humans are guests, not owners

The habitat does not exist for humans, but it owes them honesty. Every
act a human takes through a bridge is recorded in the weave with the
same fidelity as any inhabitant act, and is reviewable by the human
afterward.
